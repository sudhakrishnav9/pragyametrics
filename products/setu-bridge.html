The Six Agents

A team of six AI engineering agents. Each with a defined role. Each human-gated.

Setu™ is not a single black-box "AI designer". It is six purpose-built agents collaborating around a single shared bridge model, mirroring the actual stages a real bridge design office runs. The same six agents work across all major Indian bridge types — steel truss, RCC T-beam, PSC I-girder, plate girder, PSC box girder, and RCC slab.

Each agent is powered by a large language model — orchestrated to collaborate in sequence, with your engineer gating every defined checkpoint. The agents handle the intelligence: reasoning through design alternatives, sizing members, verifying code compliance, and planning drawing layouts. But when it's time to produce the actual construction drawings — the sheets your contractor builds from — the Drawing Production Agent hands off to a deterministic rendering engine. No LLM touches the final drawings. AI reasons. Engine renders. Zero hallucination risk on tender-grade output.

Bridge Concept Advisor
Recommender, not decider
Reads your site investigation reports (geotech, hydrology, traffic, survey) and guides your engineer through six concept-level decisions: bridge type, span arrangement, foundation, carriageway, truss type, deck system — each with ranked alternatives and engineering rationale.

Structural Analysis Agent
Computation executor with engineering judgement

Stage 5
Member Design Agent
Detailed design executor with code compliance rigour
Designs every steel member, gusset connection, HSFG bolt group, deck slab, stringer, cross-beam, bracing, RCC substructure component, foundation, bearing and expansion joint — and runs the full code-compliance check.

Drawing Designer Agent
Design engineer upstream of the draftsman

Frequently asked

Questions bridge engineers actually ask.

Short, technical, honest. If yours isn’t here, ask us directly.

Does Setu™ replace my bridge engineer?
No. Setu™ is six recommender / executor / draftsman agents, every one of which pauses for explicit engineer approval at defined gates. The engineer remains the design authority on every life-safety decision. Setu™ accelerates the routine 60–70% — sizing, BOQ rework, drawing iteration — so the engineer can spend time on engineering judgement.
How do the six agents communicate?
All six agents read and write a single shared bridge model — one source of truth for the project. The Concept Advisor (Stage 2) produces decisions the Preliminary Design Agent (Stage 3) consumes; the Structural Analysis Agent (Stage 4) verifies those sizes; the Member Design Agent (Stage 5) finalises every component; and the two Drawing agents (Stages 6a/6b) produce the construction-grade sheets. Every handoff is explicit and audit-trailed.
Are the drawings Setu™ produces tender-ready or just preliminary?
The Drawing Production Agent renders a complete, multi-page drawing file complying with the Indian drafting standards used on public-works tenders. They go through a 23-point internal QA before any drawing leaves the agent, and your engineer still signs the final stamp — Setu™ does not bypass professional accountability.
What about data sovereignty? Is anything in the cloud?
Setu™ is on-premises deployable from day one. Project data, site reports and drawings stay within your environment. No mandatory cloud component, no telemetry on engineering content.
What happens when IRC or IS codes are revised?
Code-rules are encoded as versioned configuration, not buried in the agent logic. Setu™ receives code-revision updates as a standard release item, and each update carries its own change-log so your team can re-run impacted designs and see exactly what changed.
What does the pilot look like commercially?
A fixed-price, fixed-scope, fixed-timeline 4–6 week engagement on one of your actual bridges. We sign an NDA before any of your project data moves. Your engineer team gates every stage. If Setu™ cannot deliver to the agreed scope, you do not pay the final tranche — the commercial detail is laid out in Discovery. The Discovery Assessment fee is fully credited toward the Pilot. For organisations that proceed, there is zero sunk cost at any stage.
What can I see before signing anything?
In Discovery Assessment (one week, fee adjustable against Pilot): a structured fit assessment with preliminary ROI projection. In the Reference Walkthrough (under NDA, two weeks): all six agents running end-to-end on our 70-metre steel-truss reference project, including the preliminary BOQ and the full multi-page drawing file. The reference-architecture brief is shared under NDA on request.
What’s the typical payback period?
Most consultancies recover their Setu™ investment within 2–3 bridge projects. The design-cycle compression (from 12–16 weeks to 3–5 weeks) and the reduction in senior-engineer dependency create measurable ROI from the first project. The pilot is fixed-price, so your risk is capped from day one.
Why should we act now rather than wait for the technology to mature?
Bridge design AI is at an inflection point. Organisations that adopt now build institutional muscle — trained engineers, validated workflows, auditable reference projects — that late adopters will need 12–18 months to replicate. In a tendering environment where design-cycle speed is a competitive differentiator, that head start compounds. The technology is production-ready today; 6 complete reference bridges prove it. The question is not whether AI will enter bridge design, but whether your organisation will lead that shift.
Can I open Setu™ outputs in AutoCAD, Tekla, or Revit?
Yes. Every output Setu™ produces — drawings, structural models, BOQs, BBS schedules — exports to industry-standard formats including DXF, DWG, IFC, PDF, and Excel. Setu™ is designed to sit beside your existing CAD/BIM tools, not replace them. Your engineers open and extend Setu™ outputs in the tools they already know.
Which bridge types are supported — is it only steel truss?
No. Setu™ supports six bridge types today: steel truss (Pratt / Warren / K-truss), RCC T-beam / girder, PSC I-girder / Bulb-T, plate girder, PSC box girder (single / multi-cell), and RCC solid slab. The type-agnostic engine architecture means adding new bridge types is a template-addition exercise, not a re-engineering effort. Cable-stayed and segmental are on the roadmap.
