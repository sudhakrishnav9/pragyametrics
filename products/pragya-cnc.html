Frequently asked

Questions plant managers actually ask.

Short, technical, honest. If yours isn't here, ask us directly.

Will the AI ever write to my machine, change a parameter, or clear an alarm?
No. Read-only by design is enforced at the architecture level — not a configuration that can be turned off. The connectivity layer only exposes read operations: there is no write path, no parameter change, no alarm reset, no program edit. The AI advises; the engineer always acts. We’ll walk through the enforcement details in the discovery call.
Which CNC controllers and protocols are supported out of the box?
FANUC (FOCAS-based controllers), Siemens, Mitsubishi and Heidenhain through standard industrial protocols — plus generic MTConnect, OPC UA, Modbus TCP, EtherNet/IP, and a manual/CSV fallback for legacy machines with no digital interface. New adapters can be added in 1–2 weeks against the read-only contract.
Can Pragya™ run fully offline / air-gapped?
Yes. Pragya™ supports fully offline operation with local LLM providers and the cloud channel disabled. It then runs entirely on the plant edge with zero outbound calls — you keep the repeatable incident workflow, citation-backed knowledge over local manuals, alarm enrichment, and the full audit log.
Where does our maintenance data go?
Local-first by default. Telemetry, alarms, manuals, SOPs, and chat history stay in the on-prem database. An optional cloud publisher can push specific, configurable enriched events northbound — into your existing IIoT platform or your own backplane. Nothing leaves the plant unless you turn the publisher on.
How long is a typical Pilot, and what does it cost?
A standard cell-level Pilot runs 8–10 weeks against one machining cell. The deliverable is a fully working Guided Incident capability plus Live Monitoring on real plant data, with an engineer-validated impact assessment. Pricing is fixed-price and depends on cell complexity, controller mix, and integration scope. Discovery begins with a fixed-scope Plant Readiness Assessment (typically Week 1) and an executive readiness brief with a scoped pilot recommendation—before any cell-level Pilot investment.
How is Pragya™ different from our existing IIoT platform / MES / CMMS?
Pragya™ is complementary, not competing. Most IIoT platforms operate at the cloud/fleet level. MES/CMMS systems handle work orders and production tracking. Pragya™ fills the gap they all leave: the plant-floor, controller-aware, citation-backed maintenance reasoning layer. It can publish enriched diagnostic events northbound into any of them via the optional cloud publisher.
Will it work alongside our existing FANUC service contract / OEM warranty?
Yes. Because Pragya™ is strictly read-only and never writes parameters, clears alarms, or modifies programs, it does not violate FANUC / Siemens / Mitsubishi OEM warranty terms. We can share a one-page warranty-compatibility note as part of the discovery call.
